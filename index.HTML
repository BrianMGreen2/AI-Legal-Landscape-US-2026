Federal AI

What's Already in Force

Binding or operational as of April 2026
Operational

FDA AI/ML Medical Devices

FY 2026 Spending Package + FDA Guidance

Risk-based framework for AI-enabled devices. Updated CDS guidance expands which AI tools fall outside device rules when clinicians can independently review recommendations. TEMPO pilot allows more flexible oversight of AI-enabled devices.

GAO directed to assess AI-enabled wearable medical devices for clinical decision support.

In Effect

OMB Procurement Rules

OMB Memo — Unbiased AI Principles for LLM Procurement

Federal agencies must procure LLMs that are truth-seeking and ideologically neutral. Vendor requirements include model/system/data cards, evaluations, acceptable-use policies, and feedback mechanisms. Model weights not required.

Deadline: March 11, 2026
Active

FTC Enforcement

Section 5 FTC Act (no AI-specific statute needed)

FTC is actively targeting deceptive AI marketing — fake "AI lawyer," "AI financial advisor" claims, tools generating fake reviews or earnings schemes. "AI-washing" is chargeable under existing UDAP rules. There is no AI exception.

De Facto Standard

NIST AI Risk Management Framework

NIST AI RMF — Voluntary but widely adopted

Four functions: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage. Increasingly referenced in federal guidance and procurement. For most health orgs, this is the de facto benchmark for what "reasonable" AI governance looks like.

Federal AI

What's Coming

Proposed & emerging — not yet law
Proposed

National AI Policy Framework

Trump Administration · March 2026

Proposes a federal AI statute to preempt much of the current state patchwork, especially around AI development, while preserving state power over certain uses (child safety, data-center rules, state procurement).

  • Federal statute to preempt most state AI patchwork
  • Protections for children's AI safety
  • IP and digital replica rights clarified
  • AI infrastructure and data center support
  • Innovation sandboxes and faster permitting
Key Signal for Health: Tighter federal expectations for testing, monitoring, and bias mitigation for health-adjacent AI — especially tools used in clinical decision-making and behavioral health for minors.
Pending

Kids Online Safety & SAFE BOTs

Would impose safety-by-design, transparency, and referral requirements for AI chatbots used by minors — including mental-health and health-advice bots. Not law yet, but sets direction: child-facing AI will face stricter oversight.

Pending

NO FAKES Act — Deepfake Protections

Federal civil remedies for unauthorized AI replicas of a person's voice, image, or likeness. Protects clinicians' and patients' identities from being cloned by AI without consent.

DOJ AI Litigation Task Force is actively challenging "onerous" state AI laws that conflict with federal objectives. Commerce is evaluating state statutes to identify which promote innovation vs. create barriers.

State AI Laws

Key State Laws in Effect

As of April 2026 — sorted by category
Broad AI Laws
CO
Broad AI

Colorado AI Act · SB 24-205

Effective: June 30, 2026

Requires developers and deployers of "high-risk AI" in employment, education, financial services, healthcare, housing, and insurance to implement risk management programs, conduct impact assessments, provide notices, and report algorithmic discrimination. AG-only enforcement.

CA
Broad AI

ADMT / CPPA Regulations

Effective: January 1, 2026

Pre-use notices and opt-out/opt-in rights for automated decision-making in employment, housing, credit, insurance. Applies via California privacy law rather than a standalone AI statute.

TX
Broad AI

TRAIGA · HB 2060

Effective: January 1, 2026

Establishes Texas AI Council and regulatory sandbox. Restricts state agencies from AI social-scoring or biometric ID without consent. Categorically bans AI for behavioral manipulation, unlawful discrimination, and deepfake CSAM.

UT
Broad AI

Utah AI Policy Act · SB 149

In effect since 2024

Requires disclosures when AI is used in consumer interactions. Treats deceptive failure to disclose AI use in regulated contexts as an unfair or deceptive practice. Includes safe harbors in some settings.

IL
Broad AI

AI Video Interview Act · HB 3773

In effect (amended 2022+)

Requires employers using AI to analyze video interviews to notify applicants, obtain consent, explain how AI works, delete recordings upon request, and meet data-handling requirements.

NYC
Broad AI

AEDT Law · Local Law 144

Enforcement: July 2023

Requires employers and agencies using automated employment-decision tools to conduct annual bias audits, publish results, give candidates advance notice and an opt-out. Many national employers treat compliance as a de facto baseline.

Health AI Laws
CA
Health AI

Healthcare AI Disclosure · SB 1120 / AB 3030

Effective: 2025–2026 (staggered)

Requires healthcare communicators using AI chatbots to disclose when interactions are AI-mediated. Prohibits AI tools from using titles suggesting a licensed clinician is providing care. Mandates consent and oversight.

IL
Health AI

Mental Health AI Ban · HB 1806

Effective: 2025–2026

Prohibits unlicensed individuals or entities from using AI to directly provide therapy or psychotherapy. AI mental-health tools must support — not replace — licensed clinicians. Key example in Manatt's health-AI tracker.

NV
Health AI

Mental Health AI Restrictions · AB 406

Effective: 2025–2026

Prohibits developers from programming AI systems to independently provide mental-health treatment. Requires AI tools in this space to support licensed professionals rather than replace them.

TN
Health AI

Health-Sector AI Oversight

Effective: 2026

Requires disclosures or oversight when AI is used in healthcare interactions or plan determinations. Included in Manatt's count of states with at least one health-AI law as of April 7, 2026.

NY
Health AI

AI Companion Models Law

Effective: November 25, 2025

Operators must use reasonable efforts to detect suicidal ideation or self-harm in users, refer users to crisis resources, and clearly notify users they are interacting with AI rather than a human.

Content / Deepfake + Safety Sandbox
CA
Content

Generative AI Training-Data Transparency · AB 2013

Effective: January 1, 2026

Requires certain generative-AI model providers to disclose high-level information about training data sources and provide documentation to regulators on request. Can indirectly affect health-use models.

Multi
Content

Deepfake / Synthetic Media Criminal Laws

In effect across numerous states (2019–2025)

Criminalize creation or distribution of certain AI-generated intimate images or election-related deepfakes without consent. Some add civil remedies. State-specific — check NCSL tracker for each statute.

UT
Safety Sandbox

Utah AI-Specific Sandbox

In effect: 2024–2025

Allows approved companies to test AI-driven products and services with relaxed regulatory requirements under regulator supervision, subject to consumer-protection guardrails. Important for AI pilots in Utah.
